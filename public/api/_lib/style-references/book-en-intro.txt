Chapter 4. The Alchemy of the Date: How to Calculate Your DAR
Your code is hidden inside your date of birth. Let&#x2019;s decipher it together.
Formula: MA (day + month) → ZHI (year) → KUN (sum of the first two).
Step 1: The First Digit (MA)—Potential
Add the digits of your day and month of birth, then reduce the result to a single digit between 1 and 9.
Example: 23 August = 2 + 3 (day) + 8 (month) = 13 → 1 + 3 = 4.
Step 2: The Second Digit (ZHI)—Realization
Add all the digits of your year of birth, and again reduce to a single digit.
Example: the year 1995 = 1 + 9 + 9 + 5 = 24 → 2 + 4 = 6.
Step 3: The Third Digit (KUN)—Synergy
Add the first digit (MA) and the second digit (ZHI) you have just calculated. If the sum is two digits, reduce it to one.
Example: 4 (MA) + 6 (ZHI) = 10 → 1 + 0 = 1.
Result: your DAR is 4-6-1.
The last digit (1) tells us that your gift belongs to the LOGOS family. In this case, it is the DAR ZHAR.

Chapter 6. Anatomy of the Soul: How to Read Your DAR Profile

3. The Shadow Side—Where Your Power Is Hidden
This is the most important and the most delicate section.
Here we speak of what happens when the light of the gift meets fear, pain, or misunderstanding.
It is essential to understand: the shadow is not a &#x201C;bad&#x201D; part of you. It is not your enemy and it is not a sign of defect. The shadow is your own power that took fright and curled into a ball in order to survive. It is an ancient defensive mechanism that once helped you adapt to difficult conditions, but which now, as an automatic habit, has begun to choke off your potential.
This section is built in three layers, so that you can see the whole picture:
Manifestation (the Mask): what does it look like from outside? Aggression, withdrawal, total control, self-sacrifice, cynicism, procrastination. Often we don&#x2019;t even notice it—we treat such behavior as &#x201C;normal,&#x201D; or as &#x201C;the only way to survive.&#x201D;
The Trap Scenario: what situations do you fall into again and again? Why do relationships break down on the same script? Why do projects stall just before the finish line? Here we describe the closed loop in which your energy spins, finding no way out.
The True Engine (the Deep Fear): the most important layer. We look beneath the mask and ask: what is your soul actually afraid of?
Some examples of what hides beneath:
Behind tyranny and control there is often a child&#x2019;s fear of helplessness.
Behind laziness and inaction—the fear of being rejected if you show up as your true self.
Behind hyper-responsibility—the terror of life&#x2019;s chaos and of losing love.
Recognizing this fear is the key to release. Once you see the true cause, the shadow loses its hold over you. You stop fighting the symptom (the behavior) and begin to heal the root (the fear) with love and awareness.

People of this DAR feel the richness of options. Their intuition works like panoramic vision: they read the context, see hidden connections, and sense potential in spaces where others see emptiness. The Shadow Side
Active shadow—Over-Control
The drive for order turns into total control, and the boundaries that once protected become suffocating. All spontaneity—yours and other people&#x2019;s—is suppressed. Order stops being a tool of creation and becomes an end in itself, killing every living movement.

Passive shadow—Prisoner of the Labyrinth of Ideas
The power to see possibilities malfunctions, and these people get stuck in an endless sea of variants. The fear of making the wrong choice paralyzes the will. The mind multiplies perfect scenarios, but not one of them launches into reality. Ideas remain beautiful, bodiless ghosts. There is effort, there is a plan—but the space around does not become alive and lasting; it freezes into a diagram. The mechanism functions, but the life has gone out of it.
The action is performed for the sake of the action itself, or to keep the form intact—not for the actual effect on people.
At the root: fear of mistake and imperfection. In your head the idea is flawless; the moment it is embodied, it becomes vulnerable. You stay in the safety of fantasy to avoid the meeting with reality.
The Turning Point: From Idea to First Stone
This is the moment of recognition: you are either squeezing the world into your frames, or drowning in an ocean of possibility. The path to the light is to perform a single micro-action through the full cycle of your DAR: idea → form → rule.
If you are in the active shadow:
Consciously loosen control in safe zones. Add an element of unpredictability to your daily routine. Watch and see: the world does not collapse from freedom. Direct the energy of protection toward support, not suppression.
If you are in the passive shadow:
Consciously limit the flow of ideas. Choose one—the simplest—task and give yourself permission to do it imperfectly. Return to the source of creativity by accepting limitation as a necessary condition for any beginning.
Test the structures you build for viability. Complete cycles not for the report, but for the real effect. Keep asking yourself: &#x201C;Does this rule serve life—or does it serve itself?&#x201D;
